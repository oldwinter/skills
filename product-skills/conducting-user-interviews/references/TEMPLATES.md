## 3) Screener (example)

Goal: confirm fit and recency without “pitching”.

1) What best describes your role?
2) In the last ___ days, have you done ___? (Yes/No)
3) What did you use to accomplish ___? (free text)
4) When was the last time you did ___?
5) What made it difficult or time-consuming? (free text)
6) Are you willing to do a 30–45 minute interview about your workflow? (Yes/No)

Disqualify if:
- No recent experience with the target workflow
- Only hypothetical interest, no real behavior

## 5) Consent / recording script (verbal)

- Thanks for your time. This is a research interview, not a sales call.
- We’ll ask about your recent experiences and workflow; there are no right or wrong answers.
- With your permission, I’d like to record this call for note-taking only. The recording will be stored securely and used by our internal team.
- You can skip any question or stop at any time.

**Ask:** “Is it okay if I record?”

If **no**: confirm you’ll take notes only and proceed.

## 6) Discovery interview guide (story-first)

Time box: 45 minutes (adjust as needed).

### Intro (3 min)
- Context + consent/recording
- “I’m going to ask about your recent experiences. Please be as specific as you can.”

### Warm-up (5 min)
- Tell me about your role and what a “good day” looks like.
- What does success look like for you in {domain}?

### Recent story (20–25 min)
- Tell me about the last time you {did the target job}.
- What triggered it?
- Walk me through what you did step by step.
- What was confusing, slow, risky, or frustrating?
- What did you try first? What happened?
- What tools did you use? Why those?
- What did you do instead when it didn’t work?
- How did you know you were “done”?

### Constraints + tradeoffs (7–10 min)
- What matters most: speed, quality, cost, compliance, collaboration, visibility?
- What do you avoid because it’s too risky/annoying?
- Who else is involved? What approvals or handoffs exist?

### Wrap (5 min)
- If you could wave a magic wand and improve one part of this workflow, what would it be?
- Anyone else we should talk to who does this regularly?

## 7) JTBD switch interview guide (timeline)

Goal: extract the story of a recent switch/adoption.

### Setup (2–3 min)
- Confirm what they switched from/to and when.

### Timeline + forces (25–30 min)
- What was happening when you first noticed the problem?
- What was the “push” away from the old way? (pain, risk, constraints)
- What was the “pull” toward the new way? (promise, trigger, opportunity)
- What anxieties held you back? (risk, trust, switching cost)
- What habits kept you in the old way?
- What event finally made you act?
- How did you evaluate options? Who influenced the decision?
- What nearly stopped the decision?

### Outcome (10 min)
- What does success look like now?
- What’s still hard? What would make you switch again?
