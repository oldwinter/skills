### B) Exclusive pitch email (template)

Hi <Name> —

I’m reaching out with an **exclusive first look** at <what’s new> for <who it’s for>. The headline is: <1-sentence news>.

Why it matters now: <why now, 1 sentence>. For your <beat> coverage, the specific angle is: <hook, 1 sentence>.

Proof / specifics:
- <proof 1>
- <proof 2>
- <proof 3>

We can offer:
- <founder/expert interview> on <dates/times>
- <demo/data/asset access>

If you’re interested, could you let me know by <timebox>? If not, no worries.

Thanks,  
<Name>  
<Title> | <Company>  
<Phone (optional)> | <Link>

### C) Standard pitch email (template)

Hi <Name> —

New story idea for your <beat>: <1-sentence news>.

Why now: <1 sentence>. The key detail is <specific> and it matters because <impact>.

Proof / specifics:
- <proof 1>
- <proof 2>

Happy to share more or set up a quick chat with <spokesperson> this week.

Best,  
<Name>

### D) Follow-up #1 (template)

Hi <Name> — quick bump in case this got buried.  
Key news: <1 sentence>. Happy to share assets or hop on a quick call if helpful.

### E) Close-the-loop (template)

Thanks for taking a look. If this isn’t a fit, no worries — if you can point me to who covers <topic> at <outlet>, I’d appreciate it.  
Either way, happy to be a resource for future stories on <beat>.

### F) Correction/clarification request (template)

Hi <Name> — thank you again for the piece. One small factual correction: <incorrect statement> → <correct statement>.  
Source/reference: <link or brief citation>. Appreciate your help updating when you can.
