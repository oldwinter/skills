# Rubric (Score 1–5)

Score the pack before finalizing. A “ship-ready” pack is typically **≥ 4** on all categories.

## 1) Clarity of message (1–5)
- **1:** Vague, jargon-heavy, hard to repeat
- **3:** Understandable but not crisp; multiple CTAs or angles
- **5:** One clear message + CTA; repeatable in one sentence

## 2) Strength of hook/sizzle (1–5)
- **1:** Pure hype; nothing showable
- **3:** Some concreteness but weak differentiation
- **5:** Memorable, concrete, and supported by a demo/artifact/stat

## 3) Audience/channel fit (1–5)
- **1:** Channels chosen randomly; no audience mapping
- **3:** Some mapping but lacks sequencing and metrics
- **5:** Clear channel strategy, sequencing, and success measures

## 4) PR readiness (if applicable) (1–5)
- **1:** No clear story; long pitch; unsubstantiated claims
- **3:** Decent pitch but unclear exclusive/broad strategy
- **5:** Tight pitch, clear exclusive sequencing, credible proof, readiness checks

## 5) Internal readiness quality (1–5)
- **1:** No enablement; teams will improvise
- **3:** Basic talk track/FAQ but missing objections or guardrails
- **5:** Talk track + FAQ + objections + escalation path are complete and usable

## 6) Measurement + iteration (1–5)
- **1:** No metrics; no plan to learn
- **3:** Metrics exist but experiments lack thresholds/owners
- **5:** Metrics + instrumentation assumptions + experiment plan + double-down rule

## 7) Safety and truthfulness (1–5)
- **1:** Invented claims, risky promises, no review flags
- **3:** Mostly safe but some unclear claims or missing approvals
- **5:** Truthful, substantiated, approval checkpoints, legal/compliance flags
