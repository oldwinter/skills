# Rubric (1–5)

Score the Media Relations Pack. A “ship-ready” pack is typically **≥ 22/30** with no category below 3.

## 1) Newsworthiness + proof (1–5)
- **1:** No clear “what’s new/why now”; claims are vague or hype.
- **3:** Angle exists; some proof; some gaps remain.
- **5:** Angle is crisp, timely, and supported by specific, safe-to-share proof (unknowns labeled).

## 2) Targeting + personalization (1–5)
- **1:** Generic outlet list; no hooks.
- **3:** Tiering exists; some hooks are specific.
- **5:** Tiered list is beat-aligned; every target has a tailored hook and rationale.

## 3) Exclusive/embargo strategy (1–5)
- **1:** No strategy or unrealistic timing.
- **3:** Strategy exists; some execution risk.
- **5:** Clear staggered plan with timeboxes, decision points, and fallback scenarios.

## 4) Pitch kit quality (1–5)
- **1:** Long, self-centered pitch; unclear ask.
- **3:** Usable templates; needs trimming/tailoring.
- **5:** Reporter-centric, concise templates with strong subject lines, proof bullets, and clear CTAs.

## 5) Execution readiness (tracker + cadence + materials) (1–5)
- **1:** No tracker/cadence; missing basics.
- **3:** Tracker and checklist exist; some gaps are unclear.
- **5:** Outreach tracker, cadence, and materials checklist are complete and immediately executable.

## 6) Risk management + integrity (1–5)
- **1:** Fabrication risk; no approvals/guardrails.
- **3:** Some guardrails; some sensitive areas unaddressed.
- **5:** Constraints, approvals, and sensitive topics are explicit; corrections plan exists; no invented facts.
