## 2) Sales Comp Plan Full Spec (admin-ready)
Use this as the “source of truth” doc.

### A) Overview
- Roles covered
- Plan goals (desired behaviors) and anti-goals (behaviors to prevent)
- Effective date + plan period

### B) Definitions (unambiguous)
- Closed-won definition
- Eligible revenue definition (ARR/MRR/bookings/cash)
- Cancellation/churn definition + measurement window
- Attainment definition (how quota credit is calculated)

### C) Crediting rules
- Single-owner vs split credit
- SDR/BDR contribution and rules (if applicable)
- Overlays / channels / SE participation (if applicable)
- Territory/segment rules (if applicable)

### D) Pay mix + OTE
- Base salary
- Variable target
- OTE
- Payment frequency (monthly/biweekly)

### E) Quota + ramp
- Quota by role/segment
- Ramp schedule table
- Draw/guarantee (if any): duration, repayment, eligibility

### F) Commission mechanics
- Commission rate(s) and how they are applied
- Accelerators/decelerators (attainment bands)
- SPIFFs (if any) and end date
- Discount/margin adjustments (if any)
- Payout timing (e.g., booking date, invoice date, activation date)

### G) Retention/quality alignment (choose one)
- Holdback % + retention window OR
- Chargeback policy OR
- Cash-collection basis OR
- NRR/quality multiplier method

### H) Exceptions, disputes, and changes
- Exceptions process (who can approve; documentation required)
- Dispute window (e.g., 30 days) and resolution owner
- Change control (when plans can be updated; notification requirements)

### I) Data + administration
- Required CRM fields (and owner of each)
- Required billing fields (if applicable)
- Payout calculation source (system of record)
- Audit trail + record retention

### J) Worked examples
Provide 3–5 worked payout examples that cover edge cases:
- standard deal
- discounted deal
- split credit deal
- early churn scenario (if applicable)

## 6) Rollout email + FAQ starter

Team —
Here’s the updated comp plan for <role>. The goal is to align incentives with <business outcomes> while keeping the plan simple and predictable.

- What’s changing: <3 bullets>
- What’s staying the same: <3 bullets>
- Why we’re changing it now: <2 bullets>
- Where to find the full plan + examples: <link/path>
